Klusters is a powerful and easy-to-use application designed to help neurophysiologists sort action potentials recorded from multiple neurons on groups of electrodes (e.g., tetrodes or multisite silicon probes).

Klusters was developped by Lynn Hazan in G. Buzsáki's lab (CMBN, Rutgers Newark, USA). If you use Klusters for your analyses, please be kind enough to cite the following article in your publications:

L. Hazan, M. Zugaro, G. Buzsáki (2006). Klusters, NeuroScope, NDManager: a Free Software Suite for Neurophysiological Data Processing and Visualization, J. Neurosci. Methods 155:207-216.
